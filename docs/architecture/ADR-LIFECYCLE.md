# ADR Lifecycle

Statuses are `Proposed`, `Trial`, `Accepted`, `Frozen`, `Superseded`, and `Rejected`. Every new ADR contains context, options, decision, consequences, confidence, review trigger, affected use cases, affected quality attributes, and reversibility. `Frozen` is reserved primarily for money-movement invariants, security isolation, scheme semantics, finality semantics, and irreversible data ownership. Technical adapter choices are normally `Accepted` or `Trial`.

ADR-W7, ADR-W8, and ADR-W9 retain their accepted decisions and are classified `Accepted`; they are not silently changed or retroactively frozen. Their next review adds only lifecycle metadata if they are amended or superseded.
